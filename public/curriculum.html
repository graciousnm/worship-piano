Worship Piano Curriculum

A complete path from your first note to leading worship as a professional church musician. Progress through five phases, each building on the last.

🌱

Phase 1: Piano Foundations

Months 1–3

Build your technical foundation — everything starts here.

🎵 Technique

  • • Hand independence
  • • Proper posture & finger numbering
  • • 5-finger scales (C, G, F major)
  • • Simple melodies

🎹 Basic Chords

  • • Major & minor triads in root position
  • • Smooth switching between chords
  • • Chord inversions & voice leading

🥁 Rhythm & Songs

  • • Quarter, half & whole notes
  • • Simple 4/4 strumming patterns
  • • Play hymns with triads ("Amazing Grace")
  • • Sustain pedal basics
Hand Independence Finger Numbering 5-Finger Scales Triads Inversions Pedal Technique
🔧

Phase 2: Worship-Specific Foundations

Months 4–6

Learn the language of modern worship piano — progressions, rhythm & that signature sound.

🎼 Worship Progressions

  • • I–V–vi–IV (C–G–Am–F)
  • • vi–IV–I–V (Am–F–C–G)
  • • Nashville Number System
  • • Transposing on the fly

🥁 Rhythmic Patterns

  • • Whole, half & quarter-note pad patterns
  • • Flowing vs. driving left hand
  • • 3, 4 & 5-note rhythm patterns

✨ Modern Worship Sound

  • • Dominant 7th & Major 7th chords
  • • Sus2, Sus4 & Add9 chords
  • • Playing in a band context
I–V–vi–IV Number System Sus Chords Add9 7th Chords Band Playing
🎹

Phase 3: Contemporary Worship Style

Months 7–12

Develop the rich, flowing sound of contemporary worship with passing chords, pads & fills.

🌊 Atmosphere & Texture

  • • Flowing arpeggios & broken chords
  • • Pad & ambient textures with sustain
  • • Layering with octaves

🎶 Passing Chords

  • • Secondary dominants
  • • Diminished passing chords
  • • Smooth transitions between keys

🎯 Song Mastery

  • • Melodic fills between phrases
  • • Pivot chord technique
  • • Map songs: Intro → Verse → Chorus → Bridge
Arpeggios Pads Passing Chords Modulation Melodic Fills Song Mapping
🚀

Phase 4: Worship Leading & Advanced Skills

Months 12–18

Lead worship from the piano with confidence, spontaneity & advanced harmony.

🙏 Spontaneous Worship

  • • Flowing without sheet music
  • • Responding to the moment
  • • Simple modal playing

🎤 Band Leading

  • • Cueing the band from keys
  • • Signaling transitions
  • • Vocal-piano coordination

🎼 Advanced Harmony

  • • Extended chords (9ths, 11ths, 13ths)
  • • Quartal voicings (stacked fourths)
  • • Hillsong, Bethel & Gospel styles
Spontaneous Worship Band Leading 9ths/11ths/13ths Quartal Voicings Stylistic Versatility Set Building
👑

Phase 5: Mastery & Ministry

Ongoing

Lifelong growth — transcription, arranging, production & the heart of worship.

🎧 Ear & Creativity

  • • Learn songs by ear, not charts
  • • Create your own intros & outros
  • • Build medleys & flow between songs

🎛️ Production Awareness

  • • Piano's role in a full mix
  • • Mid-range frequencies
  • • Avoid overplaying

❤️ Heart & Ministry

  • • Theology of worship
  • • Serve the congregation
  • • Show up prepared & be reliable
Transcription Arranging Production Theology Consistency Spiritual Preparation

💡 Key Worship Piano Principles

①

Less is More

Space creates atmosphere. You don't need to fill every moment — let the music breathe.

②

Serve the Lyrics

Your playing supports the message. The words matter more than your chops.

③

Dynamics Tell the Story

Soft verses, building pre-choruses, powerful choruses — dynamics guide the congregation.

④

Know When to Stop

Silence is powerful. Sometimes the most spiritual thing you can play is nothing at all.

⑤

Prepare Spiritually

You're not just a musician — you're a minister. Your spiritual preparation shapes the worship experience as much as your practice does.
